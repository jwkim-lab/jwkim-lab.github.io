About

Research that bridges theory, structure, and application.

My work focuses on designing acoustic metamaterials that outperform conventional limits in thickness, weight, and functionality. I am especially interested in systems that remain effective under practical constraints such as curved surfaces, open structures, and flow conditions.

Across my Ph.D., postdoctoral research, and collaborative projects, I have developed meta-panels, metasurfaces, metaliners, and impedance-tailored structures for applications ranging from EV noise reduction to transformer systems, cooling infrastructure, and UAV propulsion platforms.

Research Areas

Four directions shaping my current work.

Deep Sub-Wavelength Sound Absorption

Designing metasurfaces that achieve near-perfect and broadband absorption by coupling sub-wavelength resonators within extreme thickness constraints.

Lightweight Sound Insulation

Building ultra-thin meta-panels that exceed classical mass-law limits through negative effective material properties and efficient theoretical design frameworks.

Complex-Valued Impedance Control

Tailoring both phase and magnitude of scattered waves to reduce noise leaking through structural openings while preserving airflow and thermal exchange.

Noise Control Under Flow Conditions

Developing ventilated acoustic metamaterials and metaliners that remain effective in ducts and non-uniform channels with flow.
